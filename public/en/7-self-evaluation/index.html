Self-evaluation

Looking back at the journey

After 12 weeks, I evaluate the journey through three questions: what I can do now, how my way of working has changed, and what the next step should be.

Clear progress

  • I can break an application requirement into networking, data, security, deployment, and observability layers.
  • I am more confident configuring VPC, Security Groups, IAM, RDS, Elastic Beanstalk, Load Balancing, and CloudWatch.
  • I investigate failures layer by layer instead of changing many configurations at once.
  • I have developed the habit of recording evidence, technical decisions, and results after each experiment.

Areas to improve

My current experience still comes mainly from one Workshop with a limited scope. I need more practice with CI/CD, Infrastructure as Code, cost optimization, Multi-AZ design, load testing, and incident response in environments closer to production.

Next steps

In the next stage, I plan to rebuild the architecture with Terraform or AWS CDK, add an automated deployment pipeline, create clearer alerts, and measure performance before optimizing it. The goal is to move from “making the system run” to “making the system easy to operate and dependable.”
